## Public-Facing Study Summary

AI is becoming part of working life, education, and public services. Switzerland needs public input on how these changes should be handled. This study investigates whether AI-supported tools can help people identify areas of agreement and disagreement on complex public issues. The topic is AI in the future of work and labour markets.

In Stage 1, participants use the Atgora mobile app on their own phones over about two weeks. The app is used because the study needs repeated input over time: participants return about four times, usually around 10 minutes each time, with each round building on the responses collected in earlier rounds. They complete a baseline survey, read short educational modules, answer quick polls, and may submit short arguments. Later rounds may show updated material that reflects perspectives and arguments contributed by other participants. These responses help researchers map the landscape of opinions across participants while still giving participants some agency in shaping the direction of the discussion.

Some participants may then be invited to Stage 2: a half-day in-person workshop in Zurich or Lausanne. Workshop participants will either use the Atgora app to validate solution and action statements, or take part in a moderated verbal discussion. In both cases, participants review and validate the final policy recommendations before they are treated as study outputs.

Participant contributions will be synthesised into the project's final report/book and related policy-facing outputs. The intended purpose is to help decision-makers understand public priorities, concerns, and areas of agreement on AI and skills in Switzerland.

The study does not use deception. Participants are informed about the study, the role of AI, the data collected, and their right to withdraw.

### 5. Baseline Survey

Participants complete a baseline survey in the app or through a project-controlled survey flow.

Survey themes:

- AI knowledge and familiarity with large language models
- Concern about AI's impact on the job market
- Views on AI, productivity, worker protection, education, inequality, innovation, transparency, automation, and reskilling
- Demographic information needed for study analysis

Avoid adding a third-party survey provider unless the ethics documents, processor list, and data processing agreements are updated.

### 6. Stage 1 App Participation

Over about two weeks, participants use the Atgora app across four short rounds. The expected pattern is about four app sessions, around 10 minutes each, with a notification every few days asking participants to return.

Each round should build modestly on the input collected in previous rounds. Participants may see updated material that reflects perspectives, arguments, or points of disagreement contributed by others. This gives participants some agency in shaping the direction of the discussion, even though the process is asynchronous and not fully real-time.

Participants use the app to:

- Read short educational modules, called Nuggets
- Answer localised quizzes or learning checks
- Vote Agree/Disagree on statements
- Submit short written arguments through the MegaPhone/Community area

Participants should be told not to include identifying or sensitive personal information in free-text arguments.

If push notifications are used, the landing page and in-app consent flow should explain that participants may receive study reminders every few days. Notification permissions should be handled through normal mobile OS consent.

### 7. Stage 2 Invitation

A subset of participants may be invited to a half-day workshop in Zurich or Lausanne.

Selection should be based on predefined engagement criteria, such as completion of the main app tasks, applied consistently across participants. Participants who stay engaged throughout the app study and complete the main tasks will most likely be invited, subject to workshop capacity and availability.

The landing page should not imply that every Stage 1 participant will be invited to Stage 2.

### 8. Stage 2 Workshop

At the workshop, participants receive written instructions and complete a separate written consent form before randomisation.

Participants may be assigned to one of two conditions:

- **App-based condition:** Participants use their smartphones to vote on and validate solution and action statements in the Atgora app, without verbal debate.
- **AI-supported discussion:** Participants take part in a structured discussion that requires audio recording and live transcription.

Participants should register for the workshop only if they are comfortable with audio recording and live transcription during the AI-supported discussion.

All workshop participants complete a short post-survey.

## How AI Is Used

Public copy:

The AI-supported system helps researchers map patterns in participant responses. It statistically groups similar voting patterns to identify areas of agreement and disagreement. Across the two-week app phase, later rounds may reflect perspectives and arguments contributed in earlier rounds. It does not decide what participants should think, does not rank people, does not generate personalised persuasion, and does not replace participant judgement.

In Stage 2, any AI-supported summaries or statements are treated as provisional. Participants review, validate, reject, or refine outputs before they are used as study results.

Technical explanation for an expandable section:

- Participant voting data is analysed as a response matrix.
- Statistical methods such as dimensionality reduction and clustering may be used to identify opinion groups.
- Outputs are group-level representations, not individual psychological profiles.
- Minority perspectives should remain visible where relevant.
- There are no hidden optimisation criteria, persuasive nudges, or automated viewpoint rankings.
