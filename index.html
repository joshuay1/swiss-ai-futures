Registration and workshop requirements

How to participate and receive CHF 60

Swiss AI Futures is a citizen workshop and public discussion on the future of AI in Switzerland. The goal is to hear from people with different backgrounds and perspectives. You do not need prior expertise in AI to participate.

The study has two parts: online preparation in Atgora and one in-person workshop. The Zurich workshop takes place on Tuesday, August 11, 2026, from 5:30 to 7:30 pm. The exact address will be shared before the event.

The Zurich workshop can be carried out in English or German. Please indicate the language you feel most comfortable participating in when you register. If you would prefer to participate in French, please join the Lausanne workshop instead.

The AI-supported discussion requires audio recording and live transcription. Please register only if you are comfortable with this. More information will be provided before or at the workshop.

It is only possible to register for one session per study. Please bring photo identification, such as an ID or student card, to the in-person workshop.

Required steps

  1. Officially register for the study.
  2. Download Atgora and complete the Swiss AI Futures module before the workshop.
  3. Arrive on time for the in-person workshop.
  4. For Zurich, be comfortable participating in English or German.
  5. Be comfortable with audio recording and live transcription during the AI-supported discussion.
  6. Bring photo identification to the workshop.

After completing both the online preparation and the in-person workshop, you will receive CHF 60 as compensation. Payment details will be collected at the end of the workshop. If you prefer not to provide banking details, a voucher option may also be available.

Q&A

Practical questions

Who can attend?

You can take part if you live in Switzerland, are 18 or older, can use Atgora on a personal smartphone, sign up for a workshop, finish the Atgora preparation before attending, and can join one workshop in Zürich or Lausanne. For the Zurich workshop, you should be comfortable participating in English or German.

How do I sign up?

Use the Swiss AI Futures sign-up form to register for a workshop. It is only possible to register for one session per study. After signing up, please also complete the Atgora preparation before coming.

When and where are the workshops?

Zürich is Tuesday, August 11, 2026, from 5:30 to 7:30 pm; exact location in Zürich to be confirmed. Lausanne is Wednesday, August 12, 2026, from 5:30 to 7:30 pm; exact location in Lausanne to be confirmed.

Is there an apéro afterwards?

Yes. After the workshop, there will be an optional apéro for anyone who wants to continue the conversation with fellow citizens.

What will the workshop format be?

We will discuss one topic using an AI-supported deliberation tool and another topic with a human moderator. If enough people on site prefer the same language, we will try to provide a moderator who can moderate in that language.

The AI-supported discussion requires audio recording and live transcription. Please register only if you are comfortable with this.

Do I need to know a lot about AI?

No. The app includes short prompts to help participants engage with the topic.

Why do I need to use Atgora before the workshop?

The Atgora preparation gives everyone a shared starting point and helps the research team prepare the in-person discussion. It takes about 30 minutes and is required in addition to signing up.

Can I attend if I have not used Atgora yet?

Yes, but please sign up and complete the Atgora preparation first. Download Atgora, open Swiss AI Futures, and finish the module before coming to Zürich or Lausanne.

Is participation compensated?

Participants who officially register, complete the Atgora preparation before the workshop, arrive on time, attend one physical workshop, accept the audio recording and live transcription requirement, and provide photo identification will receive CHF 60 as compensation. Payment details are collected at the end of the workshop. If you prefer not to provide banking details, a voucher option may also be available. Travel costs are not reimbursed.

What should I bring?

Please bring photo identification, such as an ID or student card. You should also bring the smartphone you used for the Atgora preparation.

What data is collected?

The study team is responsible for the research data. The study uses contact details for communication, workshop coordination, and compensation administration, plus survey responses, demographic profile information, app votes, short written responses, and participation or completion information. Identifying information is stored separately from coded research data.

Does the app track my location?

No. The study does not use app geolocation or background tracking.

How do I find the Swiss AI Futures module in Atgora?

Download or open Atgora, sign in or register if asked, open Courses, select Swiss AI Futures, and finish the available preparation module before attending. The preparation takes about 30 minutes.

Why does public input matter?

AI can affect work, education, public services, and who benefits from new tools. Swiss AI Futures asks people living in Switzerland to share the questions, concerns, and ideas they want decision-makers to hear. The results contribute to a TA-SWISS research project and policy-facing outputs.

Who is running the study?

The study contact is Joshua C. Yang, ETH Zurich: joshyang@mit.edu. Swiss AI Futures is part of the TA-SWISS project Transforming Competencies in the Era of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Detecting Future Directions. TA-SWISS is the main funder and Atgora is the technology partner.

Can I withdraw?

Yes. You may withdraw at any time without giving a reason and without disadvantage.
